6500 Escalation Guidelines


Expectations when CA, Account Teams, and ISBU are working customer escalations.


Level 1: Troubleshooting and local escalation.

  1. Does the HW/SW support it?
  2. Is it configured correctly?
  3. Search topic for similar solved cases.
  4. Search the cs-cat6000 or cs-7600 aliases for similar questions.
  5. Search bock-bock for TME info.
  6. Search the BU site for presentations and whitepapers.


Level 2: Escalation within CA

Note: Case owner continues to coordinate all communication with the customer as other contacts get engaged.


  1. Document work to date in TAC case notes.
  2. Email appropriate product alias cs-cat6000. Local team rules regarding alias use still apply.

    *
    • Describe customer situation and urgency.
    • Concise problem description.
      • What did you expect?
      • What is happening?
      • What do you think the problem is and why?
      • Topology in fixed width ASCII font or link to gif/jpg (no .ppt or .doc)
      • Basic HW, config, SW version info, logs.
      • All tracebacks decoded.
    • Additional supporting data at the end or via URL, exclude irrelevant data.
    • Summary of troubleshooting and previous escalation steps.
  3. Lab recreate to verify if problem is a new or unreproduced bug. **
  4. NOTE TAC escalation should be engaged by this point.
  5. CRITICAL CASES ONLY:Engage 6500 escalation resources at this point.


* Please review smart questions. if you haven't sent questions to a technical alias before.

** If HW for a recreate is not available in your lab, open a CALO recreate request.


Level 3: Escalation to BU

If escalating an existing DDTS to raise priority then contact the DE-Manager.

If escalating a high priority (CAP) customer issue then generate an email including the following:

  • Explain the customer network impact.
  • Document previous troubleshooting and problem escalation.
  • List contacts relating to this issue.
  • Explain what help is needed and urgency.
  • Send email to bdianni
  • There is no BU pending status. Case owner is responsible to make forward progress on customer issues.


BU Response:

Cases will be accepted after confirmation of previous escalation steps. Once a case is accepted, the DSSBU will provide:

  • Guidance on troubleshooting and recreates.
  • Analysis of data collected from customer networks.
  • Coordination of BU support effort.
  • Coordination of communication between BU and support team.
  • Recreates when appropriate and only after CA has made a good faith effort to recreate.
  • Periodic status updates to BU leadership on critical customer issues.
  • Participation in direct customer communication when appropriate.

After escalation to BU, CA case owner continues to:

  • Continues to drive overall troubleshooting effort.
  • Continues to drive communication between BU and customer (conf calls, email threads)
  • Take ownership of tracking DDTS.
  • Make and track throttle commit requests.
  • Continue to maintain C3 case notes to reflect status.
  • Work closely with BU owner to:
    • Provide status to customer.
    • Continue recreate attempts.
    • Assist in verifying accuracy of fix.


Notes:

  • The 6500 escalation team engages on specific critical issues - all accepted critical issues for a customer will be coordinated by the same engineer, but one problem being accepted does not imply that all other current and future problems are exempt from the escalation process.
  • DDTS for customer issues should be filed by CA.
  • A CA engineer should be part of *all* communication between the BU, CAP team, account team, and customers.
